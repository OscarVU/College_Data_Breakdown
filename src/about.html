Oscar's Macro Sketch

Design Critique:

In terms of understandability, the graph I drew isn't that understandable without additional information written, as I fail to communicate the details of each axis and explain what I'm measuring. In terms of signifiers, ideally I'd make each dot clickable and link directly to the micro page, but I don't signify that. In terms of comfort, the space of the page is compact and provides a comfortable environment for the user. In terms of privacy, we do a pretty good job keeping the information private, as no institutional names are disclosed.

Oscar's Micro Sketch

Design Critique:

In terms of understandability, I'd say that my sketch is clear in communicating its percentages through the geometric/proportional representations on the graph. In terms of signifiers, ideally I'd make each individual piece display more data when the user hovers over it, but I don't signify that. In terms of comfort, the space of the page is compact and provides a comfortable environment for the user. In terms of privacy, we do a pretty good job keeping the information private, as we keep the name of the university anonymous.

Emmett's Macro Sketch

Design Critique:

In terms of understandability, my graph has good visibility in that it includes all categories and data. However, it does not have the best simplicity because there is a lot of data on the page. Furthermore, the structure is good as it separates the admissions criteria from the college specific information about the faculty, etc. The Graduation rate is separate from it all because it is very important. Achievability is not great here simply because of the large amount of data, but the graphs themselves are not complex. It is Comfortable and well spaced out, includes Signifiers for all the maps, and includes Mapping between the data of different colleges.

Emmett's Micro Sketch

Design Critique:

In terms of understandability, my graph has good visibility because all of the information is included and it has better simplicity than my macro graph because there's fewer data points to display. Consistency could be better as some of my diagrams are pie charts and others are of different styles. Achievability is decent here, but it might be difficult to create the $ graphic with a percentage of it shaded in. It is definitely Comfortable more so than the macro too, it has signifiers within each diagram, and there is mapping between the "applications" "acceptance" "enrollment" and "graduation" and each bar of the bar chart.
